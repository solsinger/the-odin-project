Crystol's Thanksgiving Stuffing Soup

Ingredients

  • 1 head cauliflower
  • 1 large onion (OPTIONAL)
  • 1 sticks faux butter
  • 1 box of approved broth (chicken, turkey, whatevs)
  • 3-5 stalks celery
  • 1/2 bunch parsley, chopped (or dump a bunch in, if dried)
  • 1/2 TSP dried basil (about 10 shakes with a stingey shaker)
  • 1/2 TSP ground thyme (this one is best measured, but sprinkle it in at your own peril)
  • 1 TBS fresh rosemary, minced (or about three pinches of dried, broken into bits)

Method

  1. Put a nice large sauce pot on a burner.
  2. Dice the onion and celery. (Or cut it however you want, I don't care, just make it smaller, bro.) Put it in a bowl if you run out of space.
  3. Melt the butter in the bottom of the sauce pot on 5 or 6.
  4. Start cutting your cauliflower florets off.
  5. Once the butter is bubbling, toss the onion and celery in together. Stir every half minute.
  6. Cut the florets until you're happy with the size. (Or don't... this recipe isn't finicky.)
  7. Dump the cauliflower into the pot once you're finished and stir fry until it's your desired brownness.
  8. Dump in the herbs and stir fry until it's mixed together.
  9. Dump in the broth!
  10. Eat immediately because you totes just cooked all the veg and they're only gonna get softer the next time you reheat it.

This kind of monstrosity happens when you're gluten-free and grain-free. (Or fun-free, as I like to say. Everything great has gluten in it, amirite?)
